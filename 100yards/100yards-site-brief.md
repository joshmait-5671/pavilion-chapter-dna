100 YARDS TO GO — NEW SITE BRIEF
==================================

OVERVIEW
--------
Tear down the current 100yardstogo.com and replace it with a single-page site.
Two founders front and center. Stripped down. Confident. No agency fluff.

THE PITCH
---------
Josh Mait and Alex Gonzalez are a two-person growth marketing swat team.
They are senior operators (not junior account managers) powered by AI.
They deliver the output of a full marketing team without the overhead.

CORE VALUE PROP (bold, punchy statements)
-----------------------------------------
- Two people. Full stack. AI-powered.
- We build fast.
- Brand + performance. Not one or the other.
- Lean and mean. No layers. No lag.
- Senior marketers who actually do the work.

TONE
----
Cool. Confident. Earned swagger. Not douchey. Not agency-speak.
Think: two guys who've done this a hundred times and don't need to prove it, but will anyway.
The site should feel like you walked into the room and immediately trusted these two.

SITE STRUCTURE (single page, top to bottom)
--------------------------------------------

1. HERO
   - 100 Yards logo (keep existing)
   - Bold value prop lines (see above, refine in build)
   - Photos of Josh and Alex or visual that puts them front and center
   - Clean, minimal, white/light background, sharp typography, lots of space

2. THE VIDEO
   - Centerpiece of the page
   - Tarantino-style conversation scene
   - Setting: a diner booth
   - Josh and Alex talking about marketing the way they actually talk
   - The conversation IS the pitch
   - Cinematic feel, dialogue-driven, not a corporate promo
   - Video needs to be produced separately (not part of code build)
   - Placeholder/embed area needed in the build

3. CASE STUDIES
   - Links to existing case studies at https://growth.100yardstogo.com/
   - Each case study has a podcast version (generated via NotebookLM)
   - Visitors listen to the case study instead of reading
   - Display as a clean grid or list with play buttons
   - Pull specific URLs from the growth subdomain during build

4. BOOK A CALL
   - Calendly embed (or similar scheduling tool)
   - This is the single CTA for the entire site
   - Keep it simple: headline + embedded scheduler
   - No contact form. No "tell us about your project." Just book.

DESIGN DIRECTION
----------------
- Clean and minimal. White/light backgrounds.
- Sharp, modern typography. Big and bold where it counts.
- Lots of whitespace. Let the content breathe.
- Stripped down. If it's not essential, cut it.
- The Tarantino video adds all the visual richness the page needs.
- No stock photos. No abstract graphics. Real or nothing.

WHAT'S NOT ON THE SITE
-----------------------
- No team page with 20+ contractors (the old model is gone)
- No services list with 30 line items
- No "our hypothesis" section
- No blog link
- No testimonial carousel
- No cookie banner complexity
- Subtle mention of a broader network is fine, but not a feature

TECHNICAL NOTES
---------------
- Domain: 100yardstogo.com
- Current platform appears to be Squarespace or similar
- New site can be static HTML/CSS/JS or a lightweight framework
- Needs to be fast, mobile-responsive, and dead simple
- Video embed (YouTube, Vimeo, or self-hosted)
- Calendly embed for booking
- External links to growth.100yardstogo.com for case studies

VIDEO CONCEPT: THE DINER SCENE
-------------------------------
Setting: A classic American diner. Booth seating. Coffee on the table.
Two guys (Josh and Alex) mid-conversation. No intro. No "hi welcome to our channel."
You drop into the middle of a conversation that's already happening.

The dialogue covers:
- Why most companies get marketing wrong
- What happens when two senior people just do the work
- How AI changed the math (two people doing what took twenty)
- A specific client story told casually, like gossip
- Ends with something memorable. A line. A look. Cut to black.

Style references:
- Pulp Fiction diner scene (the conversation energy, not the robbery)
- Reservoir Dogs opening (guys at a table, talking, you can't look away)
- The rhythm of the dialogue matters more than production value
- Shoot on a real location, not a set

The video should feel like you stumbled onto something real.
Not polished. Not scripted-sounding. But clearly intentional.
