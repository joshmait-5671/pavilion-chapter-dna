The Thesis
Why everyone thinks
marketing is dead.
●
The function feels commoditized.
73% of CMOs say their role has fundamentally changed in the last 2 years.
●
"One person and an AI can run marketing."
There's some truth to this. But only if that person has vision, not just a ChatGPT login.
●
Founders think their product is god's gift.
"If it's good enough, it sells itself." It doesn't. It never has.
●
Engineering spend dwarfs marketing spend.
AI companies pour money into models. Marketing gets the scraps.
●
The GTM function is in full transition.
Nobody knows what the new org chart looks like.

The Wall of Sameness
Every company says this.
Nobody remembers anyone.

The tools broke out of dev.
The marketing didn't.
Developers were the first vertical disrupted by AI. But the products expanded way beyond engineering — into sales, ops, finance. The marketing never followed.
→ Audit your messaging. Is it speaking to the person actually buying?

Marketing is a product now.
With AI, marketing outputs — content, tools, calculators, experiences — should be built like products. Shipped, iterated, measured. Not campaigns that expire.
→ Build one marketing asset this month that has a version number.
Specificity over scale.
Stop saying you do everything for everyone. Pick a use case. Own it. Make it personal, not functional.
Or — are we in a horizontal world now? This is an honest, open question.
→ Write your homepage for ONE persona. Not three. One.
KILL
Kill the funnel.
Awareness → consideration → decision? Dead. Buyers enter anywhere, skip stages, trust peers over brands.
→ Ask your last 5 customers: how did you actually find us? Map the real journey.
→ Design for the middle, not the top. Most buyers already know you exist.
Teach. Don't pitch.
Buyers are overwhelmed. Be a partner, not a pitch deck. The companies winning right now are making their customers smarter — not louder.
→ Create one piece of content that teaches something, asks for nothing.
→ Reply to a prospect's question publicly. Give away the answer.

The person you need
isn't a "marketer."
You need AI-curious, fluent, creative thinkers who can build and experiment. Not someone running playbooks from 2019.
→ Hire for taste and curiosity. Skills can be learned. Instinct can't.
→ Your first marketing hire should be able to write AND build.
The founder IS the brand.
For early-stage entering the US, the founder's voice, story, and presence IS the marketing function.
→ Post on LinkedIn 3x this week. Your voice, not your company's.
→ Say yes to the next podcast invite. Or go find one.
→ Host a dinner for 8 people. That's marketing.
Process > People.
Build the system first.
Templates, workflows, measurement. Build the machine before you hire someone to run it.
→ Document your messaging, your ICP, your content cadence — before you write a job description.
The 1-Person Marketing Dept
What one person should actually do.
Week 1–2
Nail your story. One sentence. One deck. One pitch.
Week 3–4
Pick your channel. Go deep. LinkedIn, community, content — one.
Week 5–8
Build the system. Templates, cadence, measurement. Repeatable.
Week 9–12
Customer stories. Case studies. Let your buyers sell for you.

Common Mistakes
01
Copying US competitors' messaging.
You're not them. Your origin story is your edge. Use it.
02
Leading with product instead of story.
Nobody cares about your features until they care about you.
03
Wandering SOWs, agencies with no end date.
Ambiguous goalposts. No clear definition of done.
04
Hiring a "marketer" before you have a system.
They inherit chaos. Then they leave. Then you start over.
