Centene Just Hired an ICHRA President.
That Changes Everything.
5 min read · Analysis by ICHRAverse Research

When a Fortune 25 insurer creates an entirely new executive role for ICHRA, it's not a pilot program. It's a declaration. Alan Silver, the new President of Ambetter Health Solutions, sat down for his first interview last week — and the framing was telling.

He didn't talk about ICHRA as a product. He talked about it as distribution infrastructure. The comparison he kept returning to: "This is what ACA exchanges were supposed to be for carriers — a direct path to individual enrollment at scale."

The implication for every other carrier: if Centene is staffing at the SVP level for ICHRA, the window to enter the market as an early mover is closing. The question isn't whether to participate — it's whether you're already late.

Reddit: "ICHRA is a scam designed to shift risk to employees"
A thread in r/healthinsurance with 200+ comments is making the rounds. The core claim: employers use ICHRA to dump healthcare costs onto workers who don't understand individual markets. The comments are a mix of confusion, legitimate critique, and misinformation — but the thread reveals a real gap in how ICHRA is being communicated at the employee level. Worth reading if you're building broker education materials.
