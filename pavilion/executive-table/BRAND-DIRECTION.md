# Executive Table — Brand Direction Notes

## The Brief (Josh's words)
- "Playboy at peak iconic (minus the bad stuff) + The Economist @ The Waverly Inn (peak)"
- "Iman simplicity"
- "Christopher Nolan, some big white word background shit"
- "This is a consumer brand, not a B2B brand"
- "It has to feel cool. Like putting on Lululemon. Or Alo. Or Supreme."
- "Up the smartness 10x. Not accessible, not warm, not friendly."
- "Up the edge/weird 5x visually."
- "Be persuasive. Get people on the phone — they don't need to know everything on the site."
- "Tufte! Function and beauty."
- "Sam is weird" — the brand should reflect that

## Design Principles (what landed)
1. **Iman simplicity** — 4 screens. Strip everything. One image, one line, one door.
2. **Nolan cinema** — Pure black. Giant outlined uppercase words. Scanline. Type IS the design.
3. **Tufte information architecture** — Detail page: cream, ruled grids, data tables, timeline dots, table of contents. Information as beauty.
4. **Not B2B** — No feature grids. No "how it works" sections. No section labels. No navigation links on main page.
5. **Velvet rope** — "Enrollment is selective." "Not a perk. A filter." Language of exclusivity without being obnoxious.
6. **System font, not Poppins** — Moved away from Pavilion brand font. System sans for main page, Playfair Display serif for editorial warmth (detail page).
7. **Red accent (#C23838)** — Not Pavilion pink. Deeper, more editorial.
8. **Grayscale photography** — High contrast, desaturated. Comes alive on hover.

## What's Missing for Full Brand
- **The Waverly Inn social warmth** — Current version is all Nolan ice. Master brand needs the belonging, the dinner, the "you want to be seen here" energy layered in.
- **Real photography** — Stock images are placeholders. Need actual Pavilion member/event photography.
- **Logo rethink** — Josh flagged "even the logo and the stupid mountain should be examined."
- **Illustration / animation language** — Josh wants illustrations, cartoons, animations explored. Not yet addressed.

## Value Proposition (what we landed on)
- **"Sight lines"** — The core product is operational visibility into how teams at your stage actually run.
- **AI is a pillar, not the headline** — Shows up further down the funnel. Closer, not opener.
- **Not networking, not content** — "Real numbers. Real playbooks. Real problems solved at a table."
- **Up to 5 seats** — CEO is NOT included (different role in community). Suggested roles: CRO, CMO, VP CS, RevOps, your call.
