02 — Access

Six things your team walks into.

A

Peer tables by stage

Grouped with teams at your revenue stage. Same problems. Same scale. Signal-to-noise ratio that makes every conversation count.

B

Live working sessions

Bring your biggest problem. Work it live with peers who've seen it. Leave with something to implement Monday, not a slide deck to file.

C

Salon dinners & summits

Private, curated events. The conversations that change how your leaders make decisions happen over dinner, not on a webinar.

D

Real benchmarks

Comp, attainment, tech spend, marketing as a % of revenue. Real numbers from real teams. The data your leaders actually need.

E

AI fluency

Baked into every session and playbook. Your whole team becomes conversant, not just the "AI person." They come back fluent.

F

Executive Slack

CRO, CMO, CCO, and cross-functional channels. When something breaks on a Tuesday, this is where the answer comes from.

04 — Timeline

The first 90 days.

Onboarding

Dedicated intro. Custom channel setup. Each leader placed with their functional peer group.

Week 2–4

First sessions

Live working sessions begin. Your leaders bring a real problem and work it with peers. Slack channels are active daily.

Month 2

Benchmarks land

First benchmark data delivered. Comp, attainment, budget breakdowns from companies at your stage. The guessing stops.

Day 90

Peer network formed

Your leaders have trusted relationships outside the building that directly influence how they make decisions inside it.

05 — Contrast

The difference.

Without the Table
  • Comp plans based on one company's data
  • Benchmarks from blog posts and conferences
  • Silos between CRO, CMO, CS
  • AI strategy owned by one person (or no one)
  • Hard calls made in isolation
  • Exit playbook learned during the exit
With the Table
  • Comp validated against 4–5 teams at your stage
  • Real numbers from companies that look like yours
  • Cross-functional exposure every session
  • Your GTM leaders become AI-fluent
  • Hard calls pressure-tested by peers who've been there
  • Exit playbook seen 18 months before you need it
