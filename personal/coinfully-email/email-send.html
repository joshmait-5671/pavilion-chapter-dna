COIN FACT — ISSUE NO. 1
The 1943 Copper Penny

During WWII, the U.S. Mint switched Lincoln cents to zinc-coated steel to conserve copper for the war effort. A handful of 1942 copper planchets got stuck in the presses — roughly 20 copper pennies were accidentally struck in 1943. Most people assume the steel ones are rare. They are not. If you find a 1943 penny that sticks to a magnet, it's common. If it doesn't stick, you might be holding $100,000.
