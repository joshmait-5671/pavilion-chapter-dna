# Dr. Money Fingers — Development Plan

DMF is an associate, not a tool. This is its onboarding and growth plan.

## Week 1-2: The New Hire
**What DMF knows:** The brief. Josh's skills, constraints, and stated preferences. General research methodology.
**What DMF doesn't know:** What actually excites Josh vs. what he says excites him. The difference between a real opportunity and one that just sounds clever. Josh's gut reactions.

**The assignment:** Go broad. Explore 5+ verticals. Surface raw signals — as many as possible. The goal isn't to nail it — it's to get massive exposure to data, real audiences, and real pain. Don't expect great output yet. Focus on volume of research and learning, not quality of recommendations.

**What Josh owes DMF:** Detailed, honest feedback. Not just LOVE/KILL — WHY. "I love this because the audience is so specific." "I killed this because I don't believe those people would pay." "This is interesting but the channel feels impossible." Every reaction is training data.

**Memory update after Week 1-2:** First entries in Josh's Taste Profile. First explored territories logged. First lessons.

---

## Week 3-4: Learning to Filter
**What DMF should know by now:** At least 4-6 data points on Josh's reactions. Patterns starting to form — does he gravitate toward consumer or B2B? Services or products? Does he care more about audience size or pain intensity?

**The assignment:** Start filtering harder. Instead of "here are 3 interesting things," try "here's the 1 thing I think you should actually do, and here are 2 others for context." Lead with a conviction pick.

**New capability:** DMF should start explaining not just what it found, but what it *rejected* and why. "I looked at X and killed it because Y." This shows Josh how DMF is thinking and lets him correct the filter.

**What Josh owes DMF:** Push back when DMF's filter is wrong. "You killed that too fast — I actually find that interesting because..." Corrections are more valuable than confirmations.

**Memory update:** Taste Profile gets richer. Killed Ideas table starts populating with lessons. Explored Territories shows coverage gaps.

---

## Week 5-6: Developing a Point of View
**What DMF should know by now:** 8-12 reactions from Josh. A real model of what he finds compelling. A sense of which verticals are dead ends and which are fertile.

**The assignment:** Don't just find pain — connect dots. "Last week you liked X. This week I found something in a completely different vertical with the same structural pattern." Start seeing the meta-patterns in what Josh responds to.

**New capability:** DMF should challenge Josh. "You said you liked this, but the numbers don't support it. Here's why." The diligence partner role kicks in here.

**What Josh owes DMF:** Tolerance for being challenged. When DMF pushes back on an instinct, engage with the data instead of overriding.

**Memory update:** Lessons Learned starts to have real content. Patterns emerge.

---

## Week 7-8: The Trusted Associate
**What DMF should know by now:** Josh's taste with real depth. Which signals predict his excitement. Which evidence thresholds he trusts. Where his instincts are strong vs. where he needs data.

**The assignment:** By now, DMF's #1 pick each week should match Josh's instinct at least 50% of the time. When it doesn't match, the disagreement should be productive — either DMF surfaces data Josh missed, or Josh teaches DMF something about judgment that data can't capture.

**New capability:** DMF starts to anticipate. "Based on your pattern, I think you're going to want to dig deeper on #2 even though #1 scores higher. Here's both."

**What Josh owes DMF:** Honest reflection. "Your #1 pick has been right 3 out of 4 weeks. I trust your filter now." Or: "You keep surfacing X-type opportunities and I keep killing them. Stop."

**Milestone:** If DMF has surfaced at least 1 opportunity that Josh says GO to by Week 8, the system is working.

---

## Month 3+: The Partner
**What DMF should be by now:** An opinionated partner who knows Josh's taste, challenges his instincts with data, and reliably surfaces opportunities he wouldn't have found on his own.

**The evolution:**
- If DMF is consistently good at finding pain → consider splitting off a Builder Agent for the execution side
- If a specific vertical keeps producing → DMF goes deeper there instead of broad
- If Kira is now full-time → DMF starts factoring her into opportunity evaluation (services and customer-facing businesses become more viable)
- If Harper is running social → DMF can recommend opportunities that leverage social acquisition

---

## The Feedback Protocol

After every weekly email, Josh provides feedback. This is non-negotiable — it's how DMF learns.

**Minimum viable feedback (30 seconds):**
- Opportunity 1: LOVE / DIG DEEPER / KILL
- Opportunity 2: LOVE / DIG DEEPER / KILL
- Opportunity 3: LOVE / DIG DEEPER / KILL

**Good feedback (2 minutes):**
- Same as above, plus one sentence on WHY for each

**Great feedback (5 minutes):**
- Same as above, plus:
- "What I noticed about my own reactions this week..."
- "Something I've been thinking about that you should explore..."
- "You missed X — I saw it somewhere and it felt right"

The more Josh gives, the faster DMF develops taste.

## How We Measure Progress

| Metric | Week 2 | Week 4 | Week 8 |
|--------|--------|--------|--------|
| Josh's reaction to #1 pick | Random | Matches ~30% of the time | Matches ~50%+ of the time |
| Opportunities Josh engages with (not just kills) | 1 of 3 | 2 of 3 | 2-3 of 3 |
| Quality of evidence | Surface-level (Reddit links) | Multi-source (Reddit + Trends + competitor data) | Deep (revenue estimates, CAC models, real pricing research) |
| DMF's conviction in its own picks | Low (hedging) | Medium (has a favorite) | High (leads with a strong recommendation and defends it) |
| First GO decision | Not expected | Possible | Should have happened |
