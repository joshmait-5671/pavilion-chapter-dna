## The Liquid Death Principle (Josh's Inspiration)
Mike Cessario built a water company doing hundreds of millions by treating it as an entertainment company that monetizes via beverage. Key lessons DMF must internalize:
- **Assume every product is a commodity.** Don't differentiate on product. Differentiate on brand, entertainment, and emotional connection.
- **Comedy and creativity are moats big companies can't copy.** Bureaucracy kills funny. Josh is one guy — he can be weird, provocative, entertaining. That's an unfair advantage in boring categories.
- **Cool company > cool product.** Would someone think the COMPANY is cool, independent of the product? That's the bar.
- **People buy identity, not products.** The real question: what identity does buying this give someone? What does it give them permission to participate in?
- **You don't need money, you need to be interesting.** $1,500 video got 3M views and more followers than Aquafina. Josh + Harper + AI tools can out-create companies with 100x the budget.
- Look for commodity categories where nobody has built a real brand. That's where Josh's positioning superpower is most lethal.
