## The Business

**What Gary does:** AI-generated brand films for small businesses. Image-to-video workflow. 45–120 seconds. His superpower is the creative angle — he sees the story nobody else saw. (The Juan Valdez example: he opened a local coffee founder's video with her as Juan Valdez sniffing beans in a field, then cut to her kitchen. That's Gary's instinct.)

**Why this works:** SMB owners have real stories and no way to tell them. Gary finds the story, makes it cinematic, delivers it fast. No agency, no meetings, no bullshit.

**Target customer:** SMB owners — closer to B2C than enterprise B2B. Local businesses, founders, people who have a product and a story but not a marketing team.

**Geography:** Launched in Southern Vermont but delivery is digital — works anywhere.

## The Two-Film Framework

Every month Gary delivers two films. Not "two random videos" — a system:

### Film 1 — The Origin
The brand myth. Who you are, why you exist, what you believe. Cinematic and emotional. No product pitch. The kind of thing people share because it moved them. This is the Juan Valdez play.

### Film 2 — The Shelf
The product story. What you sell, why it's different, why someone should reach for yours. Still Gary's style — not a commercial. A short film about the thing itself.

**Together:** One story about who you are. One story about what you sell. Recurring every month means the Origin deepens and the Shelf rotates with new products, seasons, angles.

*Note: "Origin" and "Shelf" are working names. Need Gary's input on final naming.*

### Visual Language
- **Palette:** Warm paper — cream (#f2ede3), tobacco brown (#8b6f3a), aged gold (#c9a96e), ink (#1a1508), film black (#0f0e0b)
- **Typography:** Fraunces (editorial serif, headlines + italic accents) + Barlow Condensed (heavy/display, stacked hero type) + DM Sans (body) + Caveat (handwritten, Gary's voice moments)
- **Hero type treatment:** Stacked — "YOUR" (outline, Barlow Condensed light), "BRAND" (filled, Barlow Condensed black), "story," (Fraunces italic, brown), "told like a movie." (Fraunces light italic)
- **Film motifs:** Animated film strip top bar, clapperboard overlay on Gary's photo, film frame corner accents, rotated side text
- **Marquee:** Brown background, scrolling taglines
- **Hazel:** Animated SVG illustration (lab-style, golden coloring). Bounces continuously. Barks every 5 seconds — jumps, mouth opens, "WOOF! 🐾" speech bubble appears, ripple effect.

### Sections (in order)
1. Film strip animation
2. Nav (Gary Miller + Hazel-approved badge + CTA)
3. Hero: stacked type left, Gary photo right (film frame treatment)
4. Marquee strip
5. Wide full-bleed Vermont landscape photo
6. The Work (video card + Gary quote card)
7. Two Films (Origin + Shelf cards, dark background)
8. Gary Bio (his photo left, text right — same photo as hero)
9. How It Works (3 steps)
10. CTA
11. Footer (Hazel animated + "Hazel-certified")

### Tone
Warm. Real. Cinematic. Weird (the good kind). Direct. Not an agency.

## Marketing Plan

### Immediate (build the reel)
**3. Free First Film for 3 Businesses**
Gary picks 3 Vermont businesses he loves. Makes a free Origin film. Gets permission to show it and a quote. Builds the sample reel the site needs. Creates local word of mouth.

### Short-term (get first paying clients)
**1. The Cold Demo**
Gary makes an unsolicited film for 5 target clients. Emails it: "I made this for you. It's yours. $299/month for more." Car salesman move — show up with the thing, not the pitch.

**6. Southern VT Facebook Groups**
Post one film per week in a different local group. Never pitch — just "made this for a local business, thought this community would appreciate it." Post Tuesday mornings. Let comments do the work. Hazel gets mentioned. People tag friends who own businesses.

### Medium-term (sustainable pipeline)
**5. Partner with a Web Designer**
Find 1–2 people building websites for SMBs in Vermont. Referral arrangement — they recommend Gary when clients ask about video. Gary delivers, partner looks good. This is the right play for someone bad at follow-up: someone else does the selling.

**4. Vermont Small Business Networks**
Gary shows up at one chamber event a month, on his e-bike with Hazel. Plays a 60-second film. Answers "how'd you make that?" He's the most interesting person in the room.

### Long game (compound)
**2. Hazel Reviews Your Brand**
Short weekly Reels/TikToks. Gary holds up a logo or product in front of Hazel. Tail wag = approved. Ignores it = not approved. 15 seconds, funny, repeatable. Ends with "if Hazel didn't approve, I can fix that."
