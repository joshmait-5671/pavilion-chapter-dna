Manchester Music Festival · 2026 Season

A few questions
for David.

We're building a subscriber experience — something to help our audience connect more deeply with the music before and after each concert. We need your thinking to make it real. Ten questions. No wrong answers. Go as long or short as you want.

01 / 10
The Season as a Whole

If you had to give this season a single sentence — not a marketing line, but the honest artistic intention — what would it be?

02 / 10
Programming Logic

Walk us through how you chose these specific concerts and pieces. What's the thread connecting them — even if it's invisible to the audience?

03 / 10
The Surprise

Which piece or moment in this season do you think will most surprise an educated but non-professional listener? What will they discover that they didn't expect?

04 / 10
The Misunderstood

Is there a piece or composer this season that you feel is commonly heard on autopilot — too familiar, too assumed? What do most people miss?

05 / 10
The Human Story

Which composer's personal circumstances — their life, their politics, their time — most directly shaped what we're going to hear? Tell us the story.

06 / 10
The Moment

In the entire season, what is the single passage or moment you most want the audience leaning forward for? Why that moment?

07 / 10
What to Listen For

If you could give subscribers one listening instruction before each concert — something specific to listen for — what would it be? Answer per concert, or focus on the one you feel most strongly about.

08 / 10
Context That Changes Everything

Is there a historical or cultural context — about America, about when these pieces were written, about what was happening in the world — that you think transforms how a listener receives the music?

09 / 10
The Audience

Our subscribers are serious, older, deeply committed to this music. What do you think they already know and take for granted — and what do you wish they'd approach with fresh ears?

10 / 10
The Drive Home

What do you want subscribers saying to each other on the drive home? Not about the performance — about what the music made them think or feel.
